Jupyter Notebooks

I like to use Jupyter Notebooks in most of my teaching. They allow students to "play around" with some of the data or some of the parameters in equations or models used in the class and visualize the results, all while learning transferrable skills such as writing Markdown and Python code, making plots and typesetting math in \(\LaTeX\).

Here are some examples:
  • an upper-level undergraduate lecture that I gave on climate sensitivity and feedbacks: HTML, Google Colab
  • an undergraduate homework assignment and solutions: HTML, Google Colab
  • an interactive tutorial for researchers, on ICESat-2 satellite data visualization and contextualization with OpenAltimetry and Google Earth Engine: GitHub (part of the 2023 ICESat-2 cloud computing hackweek, runs on CryoCloud)
